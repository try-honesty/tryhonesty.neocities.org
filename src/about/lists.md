## Software preferences
### Operating systems
Windows boy? Someone's a little Windows boy? (I'm a little Windows boy (gender neutral.))<br/>
I don't give a shit one way or the other; I think NT, XNU, and Linux should all exist,<br/>
becuase none of them are inherently 'better' than one another at the end of the day.<br/>
As I currently write this, I'm using Windows 11 on my 'workstation' system (Lenovo LOQ),<br/>
on my handheld PC where I play most games (ROG Ally), and my tablet I use for media and drawing (Surface Pro 7).<br/>
When using Linux in other environments... well, I suppose Podman on Windows uses RHEL/Fedora in WSL, but I don't dislike<br/>
Ubuntu and Debian, either. I also think openSUSE is cool, but it doesn't have enough real backing like the former two<br/>
from a community; it's very much held up by SUSE, almost entirely.<br/><br/>
I have used macOS before, and I like it quite a lot, but I don't have Apple hardware money these days.<br/>
BSD is cool, and I wish it had more use (namely for desktop),<br/>
since I like the philosophy of BSD-licenced software much more than that of GPL-licenced software a la Linux, but such is life.<br/><br/>

### Desktop environments and window managers
When diving into the hell that is the Linux desktop (I'm being tongue-in-cheek, put down the pitchfork),<br/>
I can only stand KDE Plasma and GNOME (and even then, I'd only use KDE myself,<br/>
because GTK is a nightmare toolkit from hell [pitchforks down, again]),<br/>
largely because they're currently the only ones that actually care about moving forward,<br/>
namely in that they not only bother to have Wayland support at all, but that it's the default.<br/>
There's many smaller DEs and WMs that I like, but they all have their own issues<br/>
(mostly having no roadmap towards Wayland, poor-to-non-existent HiDPI suport,<br/>
or just not being maintained at all to begin with), so they have no purpose other than a curio, but curios I like nonetheless.<br/>
Those, for me, would include MATE, Trinity, Unity, Xfce, MaXX Interactive Desktop, IceWM and Window Maker.<br/>
Old ones that clearly are out of step with them times still have a place in my heart, too; like CDE, ROX Desktop, and the original; twm.<br/><br/>
